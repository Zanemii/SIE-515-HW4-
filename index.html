Business Dining Etiquette 101
Learn how to make a great impression with us!

Introduction

Business dining etiquette is about understanding the unspoken rules that guide behavior at professional meals. From knowing where to place your napkin to navigating small talk, these practices help create a respectful and comfortable atmosphere for everyone at the table. Good dining etiquette is a powerful tool for making a strong first impression and building valuable professional relationships.

Why It Matters: In professional settings, dining etiquette reflects your awareness of social norms and respect for others. It shows that you are considerate, adaptable, and attentive to detail—all qualities that matter to future employers, clients, and colleagues.

Essentials

  • Arrive on Time: Punctuality is essential in any professional setting. Arriving a few minutes early demonstrates respect for your host's time.

  • Order Modestly: When choosing your meal, pick something mid-priced, not the most expensive item, and avoid messy foods like spaghetti.

  • Follow Table Manners: Wait to begin until everyone is served, use utensils properly, and keep distractions like phones out of sight.

  • Engage in Polite Conversation: Stick to light, professional topics and avoid controversial topics like politics or religion.

Do's and Don'ts

Do's
  • Wait for everyone to be served before starting.
  • Be polite and use courteous language like “please” and “thank you.”
  • Follow the host's lead when it comes to ordering and eating.
  • Engage in active listening and maintain eye contact.
Don'ts
  • Avoid checking your phone during the meal.
  • Do not interrupt others while they are speaking.
  • Avoid ordering messy foods that are difficult to eat neatly.
  • Do not discuss sensitive topics like politics or religion.
